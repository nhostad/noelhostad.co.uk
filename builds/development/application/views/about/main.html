"Why?" is often the most important question.

Why do I choose to do what I do? Well, frankly I think it's the most creatively satisfying work there is, or at least it is for me. Taking a project from inception to completion, with all the unexpected problems and their solutions is a lot of fun but what the end user takes from it is even better: a client that's able to improve the way they work using your system or a student that finally got a difficult concept because of the way you helped them interact with a simulation. These are the reasons I do the work I do.

What have I been doing?

I've been working in a variety of digital media for the past 10 years. Starting out as a web designer, I've gradually moved more toward a development skillset. Along the way I've also transferred a lot of this design and development mix into e-learning content. I've built on every level of depth of interaction from very basic information-only courses to simulations of complex manufactoring technology.
